ENFP-A

Enthusiastic, creative and sociable free spirits, who can always find a reason to smile.

Introduction

Campaigners are fiercely independent, and much more than stability and security, they crave creativity and freedom.
  • The Campaigner personality is a true free spirit.
  • They tend to see life as a big, complex puzzle where everything is connected
  • They need to know that they have the freedom to be innovative

Strengths & Weaknesses

Campaigners are the least likely personality type to want to trade excitement for control in their lives.
  • Don’t hesitate to step out of their comfort zones
  • Every move and every idea is part of something bigger
  • Campaigners don’t take things at face value

Friendships

As with other relationships, Campaigners view their friendships as opportunities to experience another angle of the universe, and tend to imbue potential friendships with that weighty and idealistic quality.
  • Their ability to tune into others and speak their language
  • Put their whole hearts into their friendships
  • Hold their friends in an unrealistic light

Career Paths

If there’s a challenge Campaigners face when selecting a career, it isn’t that they lack talent or options or drive, it’s that there are so many things out there that are just cool.
  • Need to feel like they’re pushing boundaries and exploring ideas
  • No one is better equipped to merge broad interests than talented and future-minded Campaigners
  • They are in constant development, shifting, presenting new angles and new approaches

Workplace Habits

But, if managers are able to maintain a spirit of guidance and camaraderie instead of "bossiness", they will find loyal and devoted contributors in their Campaigner subordinates.
  • The chance to conduct that exploration alongside other people who share their excitement
  • They’ll impress their managers with their creativity and adaptability
  • Letting their attention slip from one project to the next before they’ve dotted their I’s and crossed their T’s
